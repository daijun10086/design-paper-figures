# AnyRecon Teaser concept handoff

## Evidence mode

Primary mode: **Intuition + Results**.

The intended one-sentence takeaway is: *AnyRecon turns arbitrary sparse captures into consistent long-range 3D scenes by repeatedly grounding video diffusion in an updated 3D Geometry Memory.*

The center keeps only the minimum loop needed to explain the novelty. The right side receives most of the area because the Teaser must ultimately be carried by real interpolation, extrapolation, and large-scene outputs. A compact metric strip provides credibility without turning the Teaser into a full benchmark table.

## Reading order

1. Arbitrary and unordered sparse captures.
2. Geometry Memory retrieves useful views and grounds video diffusion.
3. A new trajectory segment is generated and fed back to strengthen the scene memory.
4. Real output slots demonstrate interpolation, extrapolation, and a 200+ frame large-scene trajectory.
5. Verified result chips summarize quality and efficiency.

## Real assets required before finalization

1. Three visually distinct sparse capture views and their camera poses.
2. One representative Geometry Memory point-cloud rendering.
3. Five matched frames from a long large-scene trajectory.
4. Three matched frames for an interpolation case.
5. Three matched frames for an extrapolation case.
6. Confirmation that the displayed PSNR/time values correspond to the selected visual case or should remain clearly labeled as aggregate benchmark results.

For case selection, prefer scientifically representative scenes whose dominant colors and brightness differ across the three result bands. Do not recolor outputs or omit difficult cases solely for aesthetics.
